### Types of Governments
- Anarchy
- Autocracy (Dictatorship, Monarchy)
- Oligarchy (Aristocracy, Theocracy)
- Democracy (Direct Representative)
	- Traditional Democratic Theory (Participatory): Government by the consent of the governed (House of Representative)
	- Pluralist Theory: Competing in politics with interest groups promoting its own interests (Faction, i.e. NRA)
	- Elitism Theory: A powerful elite rules in its own self interest (i.e. Techno feudalism, Senate, Electoral College)
### Enlightenment Thinkers
**John Locke**: Believed in natural, God-given rights, and social contract between a democratic government and the people.
**Thomas Hobbes**: People are naturally evil and selfish. Role of Government is broad - people trade rights for protection
**Baron de Montesquieu**: Power should be divided to prevent tyranny. Origin of "Checks and Balances"

### English Heritage
**Magna Carta**: Limited monarchy to guarantee nobles rights and protections

### Colonial Independence
- Conflict with Great Britain and self-reliance shaped the mindset of colonists at the time.
- Colonial Charters to run early settlements
- Virginia House of Burgesses
- The Mayflower Compact 
- British Policies and rebellion (Acts, Boycotts, Boston Massacre)
- The Second Continental Congress (Writing of the Declaration of Independence and Articles of Confederation)

### Declaration of Independence
- Primarily written by Thomas Jefferson
- Based largely on Locke's ideas 
- Political theory, list of grievances, colonial unity for independence
#### Five Themes of the Declaration of Independence
1. Limited Government
2. Popular Sovereignty
3. Republicanism
4. Natural Rights (Natural Law)
5. Social Contract

## Articles of Confederation
- The first government formed in the United States
- Paranoia over strong central governments led the colonists to create this constitution
- It created a weak national government that was subordinate to the states
- Each state had its own sovereignty, with the national government being weak intentionally 
- All national authority belonged to a congress that was represented by the thirteen states and each state had an equal vote
- National gov could not tax or raise its own army
#### Weaknesses of the Articles of Confederation
- one vote for each state, regardless of size
- Congress powerless to lay and collect taxes or duties
- Congress powerless to regulate foreign and interstate commerce
- No executive to enforce act of Congress
- No national court system
